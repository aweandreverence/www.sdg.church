# Bibliography & Resources

Resources that inform editorial decisions for sdg.church. For inclusion criteria and theological framework, see [EDITORIAL_GUIDELINES.md](EDITORIAL_GUIDELINES.md).

## Theological & Editorial Frameworks

These works shape *how we decide* who to include or exclude.

| Resource | Author | Why It Matters |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| [Charlottesville, Confederate Memorials, and Southern Culture](https://www.desiringgod.org/articles/charlottesville-confederate-memorials-and-southern-culture) | John Piper (Desiring God, 2017) | Provides a 3-question framework for evaluating whether to memorialize historical figures: (1) What reality is being memorialized? (2) Is it worthy of admiration? (3) Is the person too compromised with evil? Applied in our evaluation of Confederate-era figures (see Case Studies in EDITORIAL_GUIDELINES.md). |
| *The Civil War as a Theological Crisis* | Mark Noll | Essential context for evaluating Civil War-era Christians. Demonstrates how slavery was defended on theological grounds — relevant to our inclusion criteria for figures from that period. |
| [The Christian Faith of Florence Nightingale](https://www.premierchristianity.com/home/the-christian-faith-of-florence-nightingale-the-founder-of-modern-nursing/2827.article) | Premier Christianity | Key source for evaluating Nightingale. Documents her denial of miracles, rejection of hell, and heterodox background. Used in our decision to exclude her (see Case Studies). |

## Reference Works

General references for research and fact-checking (not sourcing tools).

- **[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/)** — Starting point for biographical facts, dates, and sourcing. Always verify against primary sources.
- **[CCEL (Christian Classics Ethereal Library)](https://www.ccel.org/)** — Free access to primary writings of historical Christians.
- **[Theopedia](https://www.theopedia.com/)** — Reformed-leaning theological encyclopedia.
- **[Wikimedia Commons](https://commons.wikimedia.org/)** — Source for public domain portrait images.

## How to Update This File

When a new source is reviewed for candidate sourcing:
1. Add it to the **Candidate Sourcing** table with a description and review status
2. Note what it yielded (number of additions, PR numbers, Linear task IDs for pending candidates)
3. Update the status as the review progresses

When a theological resource informs an editorial decision:
1. Add it to the **Theological & Editorial Frameworks** table
2. Reference it in the relevant Case Study in EDITORIAL_GUIDELINES.md
